Meeting Guide

Talking points, objection handlers, and closing strategy for your meeting with Andreas Steiner (CFO) and Mario Curcic

Opening Frame (2 minutes)

Set the tone: you've done the homework together, today is about alignment and next steps.

"Andreas, Mario – thank you for the time you've invested in this process. Based on our discovery sessions and the data your team provided, we've refined our approach to focus on the two use cases with the clearest path to ROI. Today I'd like to walk through our validation of the business case, confirm we're aligned on scope and investment, and discuss next steps to get started."

Key Message

The Core Pitch

"We've done the homework together. Today is about confirming we're aligned on the value, the approach, and the path forward."

For Andreas (CFO)

Focus on working capital impact, payback period, and risk mitigation through pilot structure

For Mario (COE Lead)

Emphasize operational efficiency, team productivity gains, and vendor satisfaction improvement

Common Objections & Responses

Click each objection to see the recommended response.

"The budget is higher than expected" +
"I understand. Let me reframe this: the $300K investment is for a 12-week pilot that addresses two use cases in parallel. If we hit our targets – 60% vendor inquiry deflection and 30% invoice cycle time reduction – the ROI is substantial. We're also structured for a clear go/no-go at 12 weeks. You're not committing to a multi-year transformation; you're buying validated learning and measurable results."
Alternative Offer

"We could phase this differently – start with just the Vendor Communication Bot at roughly half the investment, prove value in 8 weeks, then decide on O2C. But given the working capital impact of O2C, running them in parallel gives you faster time-to-value on your biggest dollar opportunity."

"Can we do this faster/cheaper?" +
"The 12-week timeline is already aggressive. Weeks 1-2 are discovery and setup – that's non-negotiable for SAP integration. We've scoped to minimum viable – ECN-focused invoicing, not the full invoice population; high-volume vendor queries, not every edge case. Cutting further risks either quality or measurable results at the end."
"We need to socialize this internally" +
"Absolutely. What would be most helpful for you to share with [stakeholder]? We can provide a one-page summary, the ROI model, or join a brief call if that would help move things forward."
Follow-up Question

"What's the timeline for that internal discussion? We'd like to finalize the SOW by mid-March to hit an April 6 kick-off."

"What if it doesn't work?" +
"That's exactly why we structured this as a 12-week pilot with clear success metrics. At week 12, we'll have processed 100+ invoices through the AI workflow and measured deflection rates on the chatbot. If we don't hit 80% accuracy on invoice generation or 60% deflection, you'll know – and you'll have learned exactly where the gaps are for any future investment. The pilot is designed to de-risk, not lock you in."
"We're also talking to other vendors" +
"That's smart due diligence. I'd encourage you to compare a few things: First, specificity of approach – we've already mapped your O2C process, identified ECN as the key bottleneck, and scoped to your SAP environment. Second, risk structure – our 12-week pilot with clear metrics means you're not locked into a long-term commitment. Third, time-to-value – with an April kick-off, you're seeing results by late June."

The Ask

"Andreas, based on what we've validated together, I believe we're aligned on the problem, the approach, and the value. Here's what I'd like to propose as next steps:

1. This week: Confirm scope alignment and budget range ($260-300K)
2. By end of February: Complete MSA terms and conditions
3. Early March: We deliver the SOW for your review
4. Mid-March: Finalize both agreements
5. April 6: Kick off

Does that timeline work for you? Is there anything that would prevent us from moving forward on this path?"

If They're Ready to Commit

"Excellent. Let's lock in the timeline. I'll have our team send over the MSA template this week, and we can schedule a brief call to walk through terms. Who on your side should be involved in that review?"

If They Need More Time

"I understand. What specific information would help you make a final decision? And what's a realistic timeline for you to complete your internal review? I want to make sure we're set up for an April kick-off."
